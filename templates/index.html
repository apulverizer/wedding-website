How We Got Here

Dawn

Four years ago, I was just a girl who had a crush on a guy who had a girlfriend. And I had to do the hardest thing that I've ever had to do, which was just to...wait. And a lot of people told me I was crazy to wait this long for a date with a friend but I think, even then, I knew I was waiting for my husband. Okay, I totally ripped that off of The Office, but it does a really nice job of summarizing our story.

Aaron and I met at the beginning of our Freshman year at RIT. We talked a bit that year, but it wasn’t love at first sight. When I moved to Florida, we stayed in touch by playing xbox together. I found myself staying up late every night just to talk to Aaron about our days. The more I learned about him, the deeper my feelings grew. We remained "just best friends" for a long time. But when I moved back to New York, I took a chance and expressed my feelings to him. Turns out he felt the same way all along. Who knew?

Aaron

I guess I’ll pick up where Dawn left off. We began our official relationship in August of 2013, but I felt like we already had years of foundation beneath us. Since then we've enjoyed sharing the adventures of life together. I knew pretty early on that Dawn was the "one", but it wasn’t until after I had a better idea of the ring that I put the gears in motion.

We made plans to do a hike in the Adirondacks on the morning of New Year’s Eve. We ended up choosing Bald Mountain, and even though we've both hiked it many times, seeing it so quiet, covered with fresh snow was uniquely beautiful. When the other hikers cleared out, I expressed to Dawn how much she meant to me, before I got down on one knee and asked her to marry me. She said yes, which made the hike down a lot easier than the nerve-wracking hike up!

Travel & Lodging

The wedding weekend is situated at the peak of the fall foliage, and might coincide with the area's Oktoberfest, so we recommend booking your accomodations as soon as you feel comfortable, so you'll have the most options available to you.

For your convenience, we have blocked out 10(5 King Suites & 5 Double/Double) rooms at the Hotel North Woods for Friday September 29th, and Saturday September 30th. The price per night is $151.20 and requires a minimum 2-night stay as well as refundable 1-night deposit (cancellation on this booking is required 14 days prior to arrival for deposit refund). Additionally, the price per room may increase if fewer than 8 rooms are booked). You must book by August 14th 2017; rooms not booked by then will be released. Please call the hotel directly at (518) 523-1818 and mention the "Jones/Pulver wedding block" to reserve a room.

Hotel North Woods is located in downtown Lake Placid, offering a central location to most of the area’s activities, shops, and restaurants. The hotel even has two on-sight restaurants, one of which offers up live music and dancing Thursdays through Saturdays. The Hotel North Woods is definitely not the only option for lodging in the area. There are many other hotel, motel and inn options located in the area. Vacation rental properties are available through VRBO or Adirondack by Owner. There are smaller cabin rentals available through the Adirondack Mountain Club and the Whiteface KOA as well, and room options available through AirBNB.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the ceremony and reception?

The ceremony and reception are at the Adirondak Loj in Lake Placid, New York.

How do I get to the Adirondak Loj?

From Lake Placid: Drive East on Route 73, then turn right onto Adirondak Loj Road. Drive to the end of the road (about 5 Miles)
From I-87: Continue West on Route 73 for about 26 miles, then turn left onto Adirondak Loj Road. Drive to the end of the road (about 5 miles).

I am coming from out of town, where should I stay?

Lake Placid has many lodging options including hotels, cottages, and cabins. We recommend checking out the Hotel North Woods for it's central location, and in-house restaurant and club. Please see the travel section for more details.

Where is the after-party?

The Lake Placid Pub and Brewery. Please mention the Jones/Pulver wedding when you arrive to get a bracelet.

How do I get from the Adirondak Loj to the after-party?

From the Adirondak Loj: Drive North on Adirondak Loj road until it intersects Route 73. Turn left onto Route 73 and continue until it intersects Main Street in Lake Placid. Turn left onto Main Street and continue for a quarter mile. Turn right onto Shore Drive. The brewery will be on the right just up the road.

Where do I park?

Parking will be set aside for guests in one of the parkig lots next to the High Peaks Information Center. There will be signs near the Adirondak Loj entrance.

Will I have to pay for parking?

No, if there is a gate attendant, mention you are a guest of the wedding.

What time should we be there?

We recommend guests arrive and find the ceremony location by 2:15, as the ceremony will begin at 2:30.

Does your wedding have a theme?

We are going for a semi casual atmosphere. We got a lot of our attire inspiration from one of our favorite shows, Peaky Blinders.

Disclaimer: We are not encouraging anyone to sew razorblades into their hats.

What should I wear?

We encourage everyone to take the weather and their comfort into account when choosing their outfits. The festivities are being held on the Loj lawn so we recommend wearing shoes you can comfortably navigate the terrain in. As far as what to wear, anything you feel fits the 'dressy-casual' category will likely be fine, but feel free to direct any questions or concerns our way.

Will there be food?

There will be snacks and platters available during the social hour followed by a Utica themed italian buffet for dinner. There is a line on the RSVP card for you to write down any food allergies or sensitivities, but you may also contact us directly with those concerns.

Will there be alcohol?

There will be beer, wine coolers, hard cider, and wine at the reception. We are also hosting the optional after-party at the Lake Placid Pub & Brewery, where we'll have a full open bar.

Can I bring a guest?

Unfortunately our dream venue has us capped at a guest-list of 55 people, including us. While we always wanted a smaller and more intimate wedding, we had no idea how difficult it would be to remain within that limit. Due to that constraint we ask that you take note of your rsvp card and the outter envelope for additional guest information. If you would like to bring a date, but have not been given an additional seat, please contact us to see what we can do. Please feel free to direct any questions or concerns our way as well.

When is the RSVP dealine?

The RSVP deadline is September 1st, 2017.

What should I do between the ceremony and reception?

Please feel free work your way over to the wedding tent, grab a drink, and catch up with family and friends. There will be snacks and platters available to munch on and a few lawn games, like corn hole and kan jam, to play. You're also welcome to walk around the property and take in the beautiful heart lake during it's peak foliage week. We just ask that everyone keep in mind that other people will still be using the property, and that the Loj is only to be entered for use of their restrooms.

Will there be dancing?

We are allowed to play music, but the volume must be kept at a reasonable level for the other guests using the Heart Lake area. With that said, you are more than welcome to dance. We do plan to do most of our dancing at the after-party where we don't have to be so concerned with noise ordinances.

Can I take pictures?

During the ceremony we respectfully ask guests to refrain from using their phones, tablets or cameras. We will have a photographer at the ceremony and reception to capture all of the memorable moments, so we invite you to unplug, relax, and enjoy the day.
